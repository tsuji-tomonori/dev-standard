# Evidence map

This map supports the skill design; it is not a claim of clinical validation. Source classes and the full cross-skill audit are defined in [`docs/reference/skill-evidence-audit.md`](../../../../docs/reference/skill-evidence-audit.md).

- `R`: peer-reviewed research
- `N`: normative standard or official assurance guidance
- `G`: implementation or practitioner guidance
- `L`: local or derived policy

Research findings constrain the rationale and limits of the skill. They do not authorize diagnosis, universal claims about users, indiscriminate questioning, or automatic endorsement.

## Listening and collaborative understanding

| Class | Source | Verified use and boundary |
|---|---|---|
| R | Weger et al. (2014), [The Relative Effectiveness of Active Listening in Initial Interactions](https://doi.org/10.1080/10904018.2013.813234) | Active listening increased felt understanding in initial interactions. Do not claim superiority to advice on every outcome or context. |
| R | Kluger et al. (2024), [A Meta-analytic Systematic Review and Theory of the Effects of Perceived Listening on Work Outcomes](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10869-023-09897-5) | Perceived listening relates to work and relationship outcomes, with heterogeneity, self-report, and causal limitations. |
| R | Clark & Brennan (1991), [Grounding in Communication](https://doi.org/10.1037/10096-006) | Common ground is established and updated collaboratively; inference still requires an easy route to correction. |
| R | Eyal, Steffel, & Epley (2018), [Perspective Mistaking](https://doi.org/10.1037/pspa0000115) | Perspective-getting outperformed merely imagining another's perspective in the studied tasks. Direct questioning is not always safe, necessary, or available. |
| R | Huang et al. (2017), [It Doesn't Hurt to Ask: Question-Asking Increases Liking](https://doi.org/10.1037/pspi0000097), with the [2025 correction](https://doi.org/10.1037/pspi0000491) | Follow-up questions signaled responsiveness and increased liking in getting-acquainted chat and speed-dating studies. The correction changes reported details but not the main conclusion. This does not justify question volume, question stacks, or asking when the answer does not change the path. |

## Core, structure, and compression

| Class | Source | Verified use and boundary |
|---|---|---|
| R | Kintsch & van Dijk (1978), [Toward a Model of Text Comprehension and Production](https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-295X.85.5.363) | Local propositions support a goal-controlled macrostructure; the operational core must remain recoverable from the user's input. |
| R | Roberts (2012), [Information Structure in Discourse](https://doi.org/10.3765/sp.5.6) | Relevance depends on the question under discussion; the model is a discourse framework, not a universal ranking algorithm. |
| R | Reyna (2012), [A New Intuitionism](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4268540/) | Gist and verbatim representations are distinct, so precision-bearing details require explicit protection. |
| R | Nenkova & Passonneau (2004), [Evaluating Content Selection in Summarization](https://aclanthology.org/N04-1019/) | Weighted semantic units motivate explicit completeness checks; they do not make one automatic score a truth oracle. |
| R | Liu et al. (2023), [Revisiting the Gold Standard: Grounding Summarization Evaluation with Robust Human Evaluation](https://doi.org/10.18653/v1/2023.acl-long.228) | Atomic content units support fine-grained completeness evaluation. |
| R | Gibson et al. (2019), [How Efficiency Shapes Human Language](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tics.2019.02.003), with the [2019 correction](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tics.2019.09.005) | Communication trades signal cost against meaning distortion. The correction is spelling/notation-level and does not change this use. |
| R | 宮田ほか (2024), [日本語文簡易化コーパスの構築と分析](https://doi.org/10.5715/jnlp.31.590) | Japanese simplification requires both fluency and meaning-preservation checks. |

## Tone, autonomy, and boundaries

| Class | Source | Verified use and boundary |
|---|---|---|
| R | Steindl et al. (2015), [Understanding Psychological Reactance](https://doi.org/10.1027/2151-2604/a000222) | Controlling language can elicit reactance; do not diagnose reactance in an individual user. |
| R | Yu, Berg, & Zlatev (2021), [Emotional Acknowledgment](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.obhdp.2021.02.002) | Emotional acknowledgment can foster trust, but inaccurate valence labeling is not helpful. |
| R | Pérez-Almendros et al. (2020), [Don't Patronize Me!](https://aclanthology.org/2020.coling-main.518/) | The dataset and taxonomy identify pity, savior framing, simplistic solutions, and reduced agency as patronizing-language categories; they are not causal proof of harm in every instance. |
| R | Cheng et al. (ICLR 2026), [ELEPHANT: Measuring and Understanding Social Sycophancy in LLMs](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/publication/elephant-measuring-and-understanding-social-sycophancy-in-llms/) | Evaluate emotional validation, moral endorsement, indirectness, action, and frame acceptance separately. Benchmark rates are context-bound and not universal user-level diagnoses. |
| R | 国立国語研究所 (2025), [「そうなんですね」の使用の拡大と「そうですか」との違いについて](https://doi.org/10.15084/0002000455) | Japanese acknowledgment forms have discourse functions that depend on sequence and context rather than a universal empathic meaning. |
