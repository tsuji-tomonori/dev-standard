# Evidence map

This map supports the skill design; it is not a claim of clinical validation.

## Listening and collaborative understanding

- [Weger et al. (2014)](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/10904018.2013.813234): active listening increased felt understanding relative to advice or simple acknowledgment.
- [Kluger et al. (2024)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10869-023-09897-5): perceived listening relates to work and relationship outcomes, with causal limitations.
- [Clark & Brennan (1991)](https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~illah/CLASSDOCS/Clark91.pdf): common ground is established and updated collaboratively.
- [Eyal, Steffel, & Epley (2018)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29620401/): perspective-getting is more accurate than merely imagining another's perspective.
- [Huang et al. (2017)](https://www.hbs.edu/ris/Publication%20Files/Huang%20et%20al%202017_6945bc5e-3b3e-4c0a-addd-254c9e603c60.pdf): follow-up questions can signal responsiveness; this does not justify indiscriminate questioning.

## Core, structure, and compression

- [Kintsch & van Dijk (1978)](https://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/teaching/1516/R216/Towards.pdf): local propositions support a goal-controlled macrostructure.
- [Roberts (2012)](https://semprag.org/article/view/sp.5.6): relevance depends on the question under discussion.
- [Reyna (2012)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4268540/): gist and verbatim representations are distinct, so precision-bearing details require protection.
- [Nenkova & Passonneau (2004)](https://aclanthology.org/N04-1019.pdf): summaries can be evaluated through weighted semantic units.
- [Liu et al. (2023)](https://aclanthology.org/2023.acl-long.228.pdf): atomic content units support fine-grained completeness evaluation.
- [Gibson et al. (2019)](https://colala.berkeley.edu/papers/gibson2019how.pdf): communication trades signal cost against meaning distortion.
- [宮田ほか (2024)](https://www.jstage.jst.go.jp/article/jnlp/31/2/31_590/_article/-char/ja/): Japanese simplification requires both fluency and meaning-preservation checks.

## Tone, autonomy, and boundaries

- [Steindl et al. (2015)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4675534/): controlling language can elicit psychological reactance.
- [Yu, Berg, & Zlatev (2021)](https://justinmberg.com/wp-content/uploads/Yu-Berg-Zlatev_2021.pdf): emotional acknowledgment can foster trust, but inaccurate valence labeling is not helpful.
- [Pérez-Almendros et al. (2020)](https://aclanthology.org/2020.coling-main.518/): pity, savior framing, simplistic solutions, and reduced agency characterize patronizing language.
- [Cheng et al. (2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.13995): emotional validation, moral endorsement, indirectness, and frame acceptance should be evaluated separately in LLM responses.
- [国立国語研究所](https://repository.ninjal.ac.jp/record/2000455/files/papers2805.pdf): Japanese acknowledgment tokens have context-dependent discourse functions rather than universal empathic meaning.
