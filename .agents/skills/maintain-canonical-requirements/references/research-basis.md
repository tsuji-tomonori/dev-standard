# Evidence-to-rule map

Source classes and the cross-skill verification record are defined in [`docs/reference/skill-evidence-audit.md`](../../../../docs/reference/skill-evidence-audit.md).

| Class | Source | Finding or authority used | Operational rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| G | Design Council, [Framework for Innovation](https://www.designcouncil.org.uk/our-resources/framework-for-innovation/) | The Double Diamond separates divergent exploration from convergent definition, development, and delivery; discovery should understand rather than assume the problem. | Separate discover/define and develop/deliver. Do not converge on the first formulation. Treat the framework as process guidance, not a mandatory lifecycle standard. |
| N | IEEE Computer Society, [SWEBOK Guide Version 4.0a](https://www.computer.org/education/bodies-of-knowledge/software-engineering) | Software Requirements distinguishes product and project requirements, functional behavior, technology constraints, quality-of-service constraints, and derived requirements. A choice may be a design decision from one perspective and a downstream requirement from another. | Prefer observable outcomes and quality constraints. Preserve an exact technology or project constraint only when the choice itself has authority and scope. Record downstream obligations as derived requirements traced to the parent requirement or approved decision. Use the version and scope registered in `governance/standards/registry.json`; do not claim complete SWEBOK conformance. |
| N | NASA, [Systems Engineering Handbook Appendix](https://www.nasa.gov/reference/system-engineering-handbook-appendix/) | The requirements validation checklist asks whether a requirement is free of implementation, states what is needed rather than how to implement it, is necessary at the stated level, and traces to a need or parent requirement. | Apply a solution-specificity gate before persistence: recover the underlying outcome, test exact-choice necessity, keep the requirement at the proper abstraction level, and require authority and parent trace for constraints. Do not infer that every named technology is invalid. |
| N | NASA, [SWE-050 Software Requirements](https://swehb.nasa.gov/spaces/SWEHBVD/pages/102695421/SWE-050+-+Software+Requirements) | Functional requirements should be implementation-neutral. Derived requirements may originate in architecture or design and must remain documented, verifiable, and traceable. | Keep top-level functional requirements solution-neutral by default while allowing justified constraints and scoped flow-down. Do not disguise an architecture choice as a stakeholder requirement; trace a downstream derived requirement to its source decision. Do not imply NASA certification. |
| R | Nuseibeh (2001), [Weaving Together Requirements and Architectures](https://doi.org/10.1109/2.910904) | The Twin Peaks model treats requirements and architecture as concurrent, incremental, and iterative rather than as a one-way waterfall handoff. | Reanalyze requirements when architecture work exposes constraints or feasibility facts. Do not use solution-neutrality to postpone architecture, and do not collapse requirement authority into architecture artifacts. |
| R | Chen, Babar, and Nuseibeh (2013), [Characterizing Architecturally Significant Requirements](https://doi.org/10.1109/MS.2012.174) | The grounded-theory study characterizes architecturally significant requirements and requirements-architecture interaction across practitioners and organizations. | Treat quality needs and authoritative constraints as inputs to architecture decisions, with explicit traceability. The architectural significance of a requirement does not by itself authorize persisting a selected architecture as a stakeholder requirement. |
| R | Basili et al., [The Empirical Investigation of Perspective-Based Reading](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00368702) | Requirements reviewers use operational scenarios from distinct perspectives such as user, designer, and tester. | Review each proposed requirement from stakeholder, implementer, and tester perspectives before persistence. Do not assume every perspective has equal yield for every artifact. |
| L | [`calibrated-collaborative-listening` evidence map](../../calibrated-collaborative-listening/references/evidence-map.md) | Meaning-preserving formulation requires tentative inference, selective clarification, factual boundaries, and concise semantic checks. | Formulate likely intent, ask only path-changing questions, preserve conditions and negations, and allow easy correction. |

The sources guide conversation, review, classification, and lifecycle design. They do not create a durable requirement by themselves. Requirement authority still comes from a durable user decision, consumer contract or public compatibility obligation, law or safety obligation, adopted standard, repository policy, existing platform or support boundary, or an approved parent requirement or architecture decision.

Implementation neutrality is not a blanket ban on technology constraints, project requirements, architecture analysis, or derived requirements. The rule is to preserve the underlying outcome or quality need, justify any exact choice, record its lifetime and scope, and place the result in the correct authority: canonical requirement, ADR, generated as-built design, current-task context, or traced downstream requirement.

The JSON shape, revision protocol, generated-document contract, classification defaults, and atomic replacement behavior are local deterministic controls (`L`), not direct empirical findings.
